# OVER MISTAKE PLOY

**Audit and Compliance Protocol v4.11**

OVER MISTAKE PLOY is an experimental interactive fiction game with isometric movement set in a surreal and suffocating Victorian corporate office. Packaged in a monochromatic Gothic aesthetic with neon red accents, the game puts the player in the shoes of Operator 451 (Hazel Alder), an employee tasked with auditing digital files amidst a corporate cold war between the Squirrel Bureau and the Rat Syndicate.

## 🎭 The Corrupted Team (The Protagonists)

The "Corrupted Team" is a select squad of Auditors and Agents that operates outside the absolutist laws of the megacorporations. They earned this name because they act by committing "corruptions" in the code of reality, bypassing rules to break the infinite cycle of control of the Bureau and the Syndicate.

- **Hazel Alder — Operator 451 (The Epistemological Investigator):** Species: Gray Squirrel. Hazel is the focused and systematic protagonist who has just undergone the "cognitive wipe protocol". He constantly questions the nature of reality, making him perfect for auditing anomalies, but dangerous to the System. He struggles with the Trauma of Existential Erasure, the paranoia of not knowing if he is a real person or just a carbon copy (the carbon paper of the soul).
- **Mirage Namu — (The Double Agent):** Species: Yellow Squirrel. Gentle, expressive, and seemingly innocent, Mirage uses this charm to disarm everyone around her. In reality, she is a cunning mind acting as a Double Agent between the two corporations. She carries the Trauma of Duplicity, struggling against depersonalization and the fear of losing her true essence behind so many masks.
- **Hyun Yajasu — (The Fury):** Species: Black Squirrel. Grumpy, impatient, and dressed in an unmistakable white suit amidst the mold of the East Corridors. Hyun has no filter when criticizing the Bureau's incompetence. However, his cynicism and aggressiveness are shields for his Trauma of Impotence — the chronic fear and anticipatory grief of seeing the system destroy his few friends.
- **Jacob Nielsen — (The Exocentric Genius):** Species: Brown Rat. Wearing a beret and a bow tie, Jacob is the voice of deduction. He exudes tranquility and humility as he studies the macabre math behind the "Over Mistakes" (system errors). His burden is the Trauma of Hyperawareness: understanding the horror of reality so deeply that apathy and nihilism become constant temptations.
- **Harumi Ayani — (The Diplomat):** Species: White Rat. Elegant, restrained, and serene, dressed in oriental-influenced clothing. Harumi is the social articulator navigating the bureaucratic shadows. Under her impeccable class, she hides the Trauma of Complicity: the crushing guilt of having made impossible decisions in the past that resulted in the deletion of others to ensure the survival of a few.

## 👁️ The Conceptual Core (The Corporate Gnosis)

The dispute in Over Mistake Ploy is not just about territorial power, it is a war of Metaphysical Engineering and Instrumentalization, resulting from the "Primordial Error". This "fundamental error" or "Over Mistake" was not a random system failure, but the tragic event where both Gaji and Andy decided to abandon their human and everyday motivations. They chose to seek a profane goal beyond their limits, shaping reality according to their wills in a sick attempt to find a cure for their pain. Thus, the choice corrupted the fabric of the world, becoming the fuel of reality.

At the core of this conflict, the world is governed by two tragic forces that cannot deal with the pain of their own suffering:

### 🐿️ The Squirrel Bureau & Gaji Sycamore (The Assimilation)
The Bureau is led by Gaji Sycamore, a sick and biologically infertile man (the "Sycamore that bears no fruit"). To escape his organic failure and existential void (the pain of the individual), Gaji created a corporate cult focused on Absolute Unification.
- **The Philosophy:** The Bureau teaches that individuality is the root of pain. Through infinite bureaucracy and rituals disguised as paperwork, they destroy free will to assimilate employees into the Great Existential Tree.
- **Gaji's Paradox:** Gaji acts like a "Black Hole", wanting to devour the collective to turn it into an extension of his own sterile body. To the Bureau, the Syndicate is a disease of individualism.

### 🐀 The Rat Syndicate & Andy Nemo (The Singularization)
The Syndicate was founded by Andy Nemo (the "Nobody Man"), whose wife was killed in a tragic accident caused by negligence in a mixed team. His trauma was born from mutual dependence (the pain of the collective).
- **The Philosophy:** The Syndicate focuses on Absolute Fragmentation. They believe that connections and collectivity always generate suffering. The goal is to turn the individual into a self-sufficient and impenetrable "Lonely God".
- **Andy's Paradox:** Andy acts like a "Supernova", seeking to isolate and emancipate the "rats", breaking society so no one needs anyone anymore. The irony is that his invisibility (being "Nobody") is his greatest weapon.

### ⚖️ The Duality and the Weapon of Trauma
The corporate ideologies are, deep down, just shields created by two desperate men refusing to heal their own wounds. Both factions weaponize the psychological trauma of their employees to force them to accept Assimilation or Singularization. The Corrupted Team is unique because they decide to unite to face the graphic and physical error of reality ("Over Mistake") by confronting their own traumas, becoming the only beings free from the chains of Gaji and Andy.

## 🎞️ Main References and Inspirations

The narrative and atmospheric construction of Over Mistake Ploy draws directly from the following sources:
- **Noir Genre Works:** The investigative atmosphere of detectives, betrayals, dark corridors, and moral skepticism (where there are no pure good guys, only survivors).
- **Blue Velvet (David Lynch) & General Surrealism:** The horror and absurdity hidden beneath a monotonous facade or everyday bureaucracy, with a strong dreamlike appeal.
- **Neon Genesis Evangelion:** The backbone of existential terror. The conflict between isolation and pain (the Singularization of the Rats) versus the Human Instrumentality Project and ego annihilation (The Assimilation of the Squirrels).
- **Touches of Franz Kafka:** The crushing oppression of a labyrinthine and irrational bureaucratic system, where the individual is swallowed by absurd forms and protocols (like in The Trial).
- **Mouse P.I. For Hire:** The aesthetic of anthropomorphic detectives (squirrels and rats) in a dense and aesthetically heavy setting.
- **Spiritual Elites and Esotericism:** The idea that top corporate echelons do not seek profit, but rather to operate sorcery, occultism, and Gnostic ascension disguised as bureaucracy.

## 🎩 Aesthetic and Visual Identity

The game adopts a high-contrast premium visual design to shock and set the mood for the player:
- **Monochromatic Gothic Victorian Aesthetic:** The game is rendered in cold tones of black, gray, and white, with active alerts in neon red (like the protagonist's monocle, reality glitches, and UI meters).
- **Isometric Organic Modeling:** Escaping purely minimalist blocks, the protagonist and members of the Corrupted Team have elegant silhouettes and refined textures (like Hyun's white suit or Harumi's oriental clothes).
- **Premium Logo:** The menu title displays the "O" of Over stylized as a nut/hazelnut and the "O" of Ploy stylized as a squirrel's head with a bright neon red monocle.
- **CRT Interface:** The game features a subtle CRT filter on the menu and in the Victorian dialog boxes.

## 🕹️ Game Mechanics and Attributes

### Cognitive Indicators
- **Quotas:** Amount of audits processed at terminals.
- **Remorse (%):** The moral weight that actions and deletion decisions exert on Hazel Alder's mind. Reaching 100% causes a Mental Breakdown.
- **Suspicion (%):** The level of behavioral deviation detected by corporate sensors. Staying close to the threats patrolling the grid raises suspicion in real time. If it hits 100%, the operator is arrested/deleted.
- **Team Synchrony:** The strength of the bond and loyalty maintained with Mirage, Hyun, Jacob, and Harumi.

### The East Corridors (The 15 Rooms)
The heart of exploration lies in the mysterious East Corridors and its 15 interconnected rooms. This floor has suffered leaks from anomalies in the "System". When players navigate here, graphical stability and physical rules can undergo drastic changes — the so-called "Over Mistake" — turning exploration into a struggle for mental survival.

## 🛠️ Technologies Used and Architecture

- **3D Engine:** Three.js r184 (WebGL rendering with a third-person camera)
- **Bundler:** Vite
- **Language:** TypeScript
- **Interface and Styles:** HTML5 & Vanilla CSS
- **Sprites:** PNG sprite sheets with NearestFilter for crisp pixel art
- **Audio:** (planned) Web Audio API for an industrial soundtrack and effects

### Technical Architecture
- **Engine.ts:** Main engine: initializes WebGL renderer, scene, perspective camera (50° FOV) and game loop via `setAnimationLoop`. Handles window resizing and layout.
- **InputManager.ts:** Unified input manager: keyboard (WASD, Shift, E) and mouse with Pointer Lock API. Provides `getMouseDelta()` for orbital camera and `isKeyDown()` for movement.
- **Player.ts:** Camera-relative movement (forward/right vectors derived from camera rotation). Sprint with Shift (consumes stamina).
- **WorldScene.ts:** Independent context system: 15 rooms + 1 corridor, each as an invisible `THREE.Group` until activated. Only one context is rendered at a time.

### Doors and Transitions System
- 15 Victorian doors in the corridor (8 on the +Z side, 7 on the -Z side)
- Distance detection (< 2.5 units) with context verification
- 'E' key toggles context: corridor ↔ room
- Instant transition with a cooldown to prevent spamming

### Camera (GTA Style)
- Third person, orbits the player with the mouse (Pointer Lock)
- Fixed distance of 8 units
- Smooth movement via lerp with 5× delta

### Environment Visual Style
- Dark indoor background `#111118` (no outdoor sky)
- Warm ambient light (0.3) + directional fill (0.4) + directional key (0.5)
- Each room has a point light `0xffcc66` (intensity 0.4, range 6) in a brass chandelier
- Unique palettes per room (3 colors each: wall, floor, detail)
